Calculate risk

The probability of 90-day kidney recovery was estimated using a Cox proportional hazards model. The linear predictor, indicating risk of a patient, of the model is given by: $$ \begin{align*} \text{LP} &= 2.653 \\ & - 0.016 \cdot \text{Age in years}\\ & + 0.519 \cdot \text{I(Male)} \\ & + 0.337 \cdot \text{I(History of hypertension)} \\ & - 0.658 \cdot \text{I(Hypertension requiring 3 or more medications)} \\ & + 0.279 \cdot \text{Serum Albumin (g/dL)} \\ & - 0.398 \cdot \text{Pre-dialysis Serum Creatinine (mg/dL)} \\ & + 0.021 \cdot \text{(Pre-dialysis Serum Creatinine (mg/dL))^2} \\ & - 0.001 \cdot \text{Parathyroid hormone (pg/mL)} \\ & + 0.002 \cdot \text{Platelet count (x10E3/uL)} \\ & - 0.341 \cdot \text{Serum Potassium (mmol/L)} \\ & - 0.123 \cdot \text{Interdialytic weight gain (kg)} \\ \end{align*} $$ where \begin{equation} \text{I(condition)} = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if condition is true} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \end{equation} and for example if platelet count is 10.000uL one should enter 10.

Probability of kidney recovery

The probability of kidney recovery was calculated by $$ \begin{align*} \text{hazard} &= 0.377 \cdot \exp(0.939 \cdot \text{LP}) \\ P(\text{Kidney recovery}) &= \exp(-\text{hazard}) \cdot 100 \end{align*} $$ where exp the natural exponential.
